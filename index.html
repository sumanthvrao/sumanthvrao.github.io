About Me

I am a MS candidate at the University of California - San Diego in the Computer Science and Engineering department. I am broadly interested in efforts to improve security and privacy in technology systems. My current research focuses on using empirical methods to measure the deployment of improperly configured email spam filters. In the past, I worked on examining Consumer mobile spyware apps to identify a range of failures in protecting user data and privacy.

At UC San Diego, my master's thesis research is advised by Stefan Savage and Geoffrey M Voelker, and I am a member of the SysNet research group.
