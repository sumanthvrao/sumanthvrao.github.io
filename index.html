Research

Futuristic Smart Home Automation System (SHAS) Center for Information Security, Forensics and Cyber Resilience, PES University

The Smart Home IoT market today, suffers from challenges like privacy, cost and vendor lock-in. While the usage of IoT in industrial applications and its subsequent advantages, has proven effective and gained the trust of customers, the Smart Home market has remained more or less untapped. The goal of this project is to develop a SHAS architecture which addresses all the shortcoming of a traditional SHAS. The project developes a future-ready Smart Home solution, that lowers the entry barrier for customers to adopt Smart Home technologies easily. The following issues are some of the problems the project targets:

  • Present day voice assistant like Google Home and Amazon Alexa aren't interoperable. They each have their own ecosystem and set of "smart" devices with which they are compatible, inadvertently leading customers into a case of "vendor lock-in", in the future.
  • Privacy and security issues with smart home technologies have always been a major source of concern for customers adopting this technology.
  • Converting a home into a smart home requires significant initial investments in terms of buying appliances and compatible edge devices.
